🌿 Spotlight Initiative · Environment Theme

KAFU Citizen Science Project — Kaimosi Forest

In 2025, under the Environment, Climate Change and Natural Resource Management theme, Professor Manyali founded and now leads the KAFU Citizen Science Project, launched in partnership with the Center for International Forestry Research (CIFOR) and World Agroforestry (ICRAF) — a pioneering initiative that empowers local communities to participate directly in scientific research and environmental stewardship. The project exemplifies how universities can democratise science, moving it beyond academic labs into the hands of citizens, particularly in biodiversity hotspots like Kaimosi Forest.

Core Objectives

  • Integrate traditional ecological knowledge with modern scientific tools for forest monitoring and protection
  • Combat biodiversity loss while supporting livelihoods dependent on the forest
  • Foster intergenerational commitment to conservation through community-led actions
  • Inform evidence-based policies for sustainable forest management

The project employs a co-created citizen science model that is accessible, culturally sensitive and technologically appropriate. Communities share generational knowledge of the forest's ecological and cultural significance during structured discussions and validation workshops. Simple mobile apps enable participants — including non-literate community members and Tiriki elders — to track biodiversity, record observations on tree health, wildlife and encroachment, and contribute real-time data.

The project has demonstrated the effectiveness of citizen science in promoting sustainable forest conservation. Communities have collaboratively developed practical solutions such as sustainable harvesting and agroforestry to address deforestation. The initiative successfully integrates indigenous knowledge with scientific research, creating a community-driven framework for long-term conservation.

10Students Corner

Students Corner

KAFU students are not only learners — they are problem-solvers, innovators and community change-makers. Through research and innovation, students are actively engaged in diverse projects spanning digital technology, health, agriculture and environmental sustainability. Through platforms such as the Science Fair and Innovation Hub, these projects demonstrate practical, community-driven solutions that align with national development priorities and global challenges.

The following are some of the impactful projects developed by students:

💊 MediRemind System

Health Tech

Developed by: Dennis Wafula (BSc Computer Science, Year 4)

Problem Addressed

Poor medication adherence among patients in rural and low-connectivity areas leads to worsening health outcomes.

Solution & Innovation

A mobile-based SMS and WhatsApp reminder system that alerts patients to take medication on time, even in low internet environments.

Community Impact

Improved treatment compliance, reduced hospital readmissions and enhanced healthcare accessibility in underserved communities.

🗳️ KAFU Online Voting System

Civic Tech · IP Registered

Developed by: Yohana Obiye · Best Poster, KAFU Science Fair 2025

Problem Addressed

Lack of transparency, inefficiency and manipulation in manual voting systems (especially student elections).

Solution & Innovation

A secure digital voting platform that enables transparent, real-time and tamper-proof elections. Successfully IP-registered.

Community Impact

Promotes democratic participation, accountability and trust in institutional governance processes.

🌿 Bio Pads — Eco-Friendly Sanitary Solution

Health · Environment

Developed by: Lucy Mbugua · Best Innovation, Science Fair 2025 · Best Youth Research, NRF 2025

Problem Addressed

Limited access to affordable sanitary products and environmental pollution from synthetic pads.

Solution & Innovation

Biodegradable sanitary pads made from locally available materials — an eco-friendly and affordable solution.

Community Impact

Improved menstrual hygiene, reduced school absenteeism among girls and minimized environmental waste.
